Animashree Anandkumar

Tutorial on Spectral and Tensor Methods for Guaranteed Learning

Location: ML summer school, CMU. Dates: 14-16th July 2014.

Slides: Part 1. Part 2. Part 3.

Video: Part 1. Part2 Part3

Abstract: The talk series will cover spectral methods for learning latent variable models. Only a basic knowledge of linear algebra, optimization, and probability and statistics will be assumed. I will start with the most basic spectral approach of principal component analysis (PCA). I will then demonstrate how extensions of PCA to higher order moment tensors can be employed to learn a rich class of models, which include Gaussian mixtures, topic models, hidden Markov models and independent component analysis. I will cover the tensor decomposition algorithms in detail, which have provable learning guarantees, in contrast to expectation maximization (EM) and other heuristics. Finally, I will cover implementation issues in deploying the tensor methods including use of randomized methods for scalability, minimizing communications in a distributed framework, and talk about our recent deployment on REEF platform.
