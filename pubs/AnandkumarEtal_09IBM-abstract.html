“Designing A Fine Tooth Comb Frugally: Selectively Retrofitting Monitoring in Distributed Systems” by A. Anandkumar, C. Bisdikian, T. He, and D. Agrawal, 2009.

Abstract

Current distributed systems incorporate legacy subsystems which may not be specifically instrumented for monitoring the end-to-end business transactions supported by these systems. While a level of monitoring is possible by observing the log-records produced by these systems, retrofitting them with monitoring instrumentation provides precise high-granularity monitoring of transactions, but is an expensive proposition. Given a limited budget for such retrofitting, optimal instrumentation strategy which maximizes the effectiveness of monitoring transactions throughout the system is proposed. The operation of the end-to-end system is modeled by a queuing network; each queue represents a subsystem which produces time-stamped log-records as transactions pass through it. If a transition link in the state diagram of the queueing network is selected for retrofitting instrumentation (i.e., the subsystems connected through that link), then we can precisely monitor the transactions passing through that link. A simple heuristic which selects transition links for instrumentation in the decreasing order of their load factors is proposed. Sufficient conditions on the service distributions at the queues are proven for the load-factor heuristic to be optimal using the notion of stochastic order. An alternative heuristic, efficient in the low arrival-rate regime, is also proposed. Simulations show the effectiveness of our solutions under a variety of load conditions, outperforming ad hoc instrumentation strategies.
