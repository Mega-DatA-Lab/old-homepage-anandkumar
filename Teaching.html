Large-scale Machine Learning Fall 2014

The course will incorporate latest advances in large-scale machine learning. We are seeing a data deluge in every domain and developing principled machine learning approaches to process high dimensional data is highly pertinent. This course will lay the foundation for analysis of large-scale learning and provide a lens to some of the most exciting recent theoretical developments. At the same time, the students will get to deploy the algorithms on a cloud platform and understand the practical implications and tradeoffs involved.

Statistical Learning Theory Fall 2014

This course draws from statistics and machine learning and is an in depth study of learning algorithms. This is an advanced graduate level course. We will cover supervised learning, kernel methods, non-parametrics, optimization for machine learning, and concentration bounds.

Random Processes Winter 2011 and Fall 2011

This course is an intermediate, graduate level probability and random processes course for engineers. We will begin with an overview / review of probability basics, and then develop the more formal measure-theoretic foundations, as well as other concepts useful in subsequent courses. In addition we will discuss the most common probabilistic models and random processes, with a view on important applications in communications, control and signal processing, as well as other fields in engineering and computer sciences.

Detection Theory Spring 2011, Winter 2012 and Winter 2013

In this course, we consider a fundamental problem in statistical signal processing—detection — and some of its applications. The course introduces statistical decision theory, techniques in hypothesis testing, and performance analysis.
